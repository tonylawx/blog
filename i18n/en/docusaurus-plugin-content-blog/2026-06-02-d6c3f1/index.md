## 0 Market Assessment

On the first trading day of June, the US market's surface was still constructive, but the internal temperature was uneven. The three major indices posted modest gains while the Russell lagged. Internally, the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index was already visibly diverging, and the real support for the indices remained a small group of AI and mega-cap tech weights. The S&P continues to trade near highs, but very few constituents are making new highs, and a large cohort remains more than 20% off their peaks — a clear sign the index itself is distorted and this is not a broad-based bull market.

On the macro front, ISM manufacturing PMI rose to 54, with both new orders and production improving. But the Middle East situation is lifting energy and fuel costs, and corporates continue to feel supply chain, raw material, and margin pressure. Oil spiked on Strait of Hormuz and Bab el-Mandeb risk before easing on ceasefire headlines, but still settled near the highs. The core read right now: indices can still be carried by AI weights, but the moment semiconductor and mega-cap tech momentum rolls over, index fragility will be amplified quickly.

## 1 Risk Warning

The first risk layer is the extremely narrow market breadth. New index highs do not mean most stocks are participating — they mean capital is concentrating into fewer and fewer winners. The moment mega-caps like NVDA and MSFT decelerate, the S&P and Nasdaq lose their most important prop.

The second risk layer is energy prices and geopolitics. If oil re-anchors at high levels, it keeps squeezing corporate margins and re-ignites inflation expectations.

The third risk layer sits in short-term positioning. After IGV's extended surge, daily and weekly RSI are clearly overbought. Within software, companies with genuine fundamental improvement and those merely driven by flows must be evaluated separately. The SpaceX IPO may also siphon liquidity from high-volatility, high-profit-tape names — stocks that have ripped over the past two months, notched new highs, and accumulated crowded speculative positioning are most vulnerable to profit-taking. The S&P CTA sell trigger has now migrated up to roughly 7232 — about 4.5%–5% from current levels. A break below that level could see systematic selling amplify the downside.

## 2 Names to Watch

MSFT maintains its constructive structure. Pullback support sits at 440–450; overhead resistance remains at 466–490. The more ambitious target zone is 498–516, but a one-shot breakout should not be expected.

NVDA surged on positive catalysts. Short-term minor support is 208–218; 226–230 resistance is not heavy, and the path remains toward 236 and new highs — though its mega-cap size means the pace won't repeat every day. AMD is moving faster; the pullback zone to watch is 442–453. Positioning is already stretched, so the short-term question is whether the flows continue.

META continues to digest inside 559–611. Without a break above 638–660, the tape stays slow; the core tension remains heavy AI capex against slow monetization. AMZN's current 260–275 is moderate support, but the better long-term margin of safety sits below 256, and below 240 is where it gets interesting. TSLA's close at 415 closes the short-term upside window; the next watch is whether the trendline and 100-day moving average near 404–405 hold. A break turns any bounce into a trim window and increases risk of a move toward 368. GOOG is under short-term pressure from a large equity raise, but the 340–350 zone deserves long-term observation. AAPL: watch near-term support at 291–302 and stronger support at 265–281.

## 3 Trading Strategy

This is not a tape to chase indices blindly at the highs. The indices can keep rising, but only on the back of a few strong mega-caps and localized flow concentration. Positioning should focus on names where the structure is clear, support is well-defined, and the valuation and flow backdrop can be explained. Core holdings like MSFT and NVDA can stay on trend watch, but chasing should wait for either a pullback or confirmed breakout. For names that have already ripped higher like AMD, profit defense and position sizing matter more.

IGV strategy is about patience. 107 has been taken out, but 109–111 is the more important right-side confirmation zone. Until that breaks, don't chase the middle. A break below 101 effectively ends this leg of upside. Inside software, only trade selective names where earnings and AI monetization are genuinely improving; protect against crowding-driven new-high names where a stumble can trigger a stampede. For TSLA, first watch whether 404 holds; for GOOG, treat 340–350 as the long-term accumulation zone. The overarching strategy: keep the strong mainline exposure, suppress the chase impulse, and move risk triggers to the front — CTA levels, oil prices, and any cracking in high-profit-tape names.
