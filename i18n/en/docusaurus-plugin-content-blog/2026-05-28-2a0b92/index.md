## 0 Market Assessment

The four majors moved into sideways consolidation, with S&P 500 advancers and decliners nearly even. The market did not continue its one-way rally, but the structural divergence got clearer. Consumer discretionary and staples led, while energy lagged — Brent crude retreated toward $93; if it breaks below $90, energy is likely to form a top, and lower oil pressure would keep improving airlines, travel, and consumer names weighed down by fuel and inflation.

The meaning of the index itself is fading. Goldman raised its S&P 500 year-end target to 8,000, with the core rationale being 2026 EPS raised to $340 and 2027 to $385 — but a large slice of that comes from earnings revisions for AI beneficiaries and a few semiconductor and memory companies. A new index high does not mean most stocks are getting better in sync. Right now it's more useful to watch sector earnings revisions and capital flows than to fixate on index points.

## 1 Risk Watch

Semiconductors are starting to flash short-term warning signals. Of the 30 SOX components, 27 declined, the SOX index's winning streak stalled, and multiple heavyweights saw expanding-volume pullbacks — QCOM, MRVL, TXN, and ASML all came under clear pressure. MRVL's earnings themselves were fine — revenue, data-center revenue, and Q2 guidance came in slightly ahead — but intraday the stock printed an expanding-volume doji-like topping formation. A close above 218.26 invalidates that warning; otherwise it reads more like a post-mission pullback to watch.

Software's risk isn't valuation — it's whether the market believes AI transformation can generate incremental revenue. CRM printed beats on both EPS and revenue, raised full-year sales and EPS guidance, but remaining performance obligation growth missed expectations. Agentforce annualized recurring revenue crossed $1 billion, which against a $46 billion annual revenue base is still small. In other words, the legacy business isn't being disrupted by AI yet, but AI's pull on revenue isn't enough to make the market re-rate the name.

## 2 Names in Focus

TSLA's short-term dividing line is clearer now. The original 420–465 macro range can be subdivided into a 433–465 attack window — only a breakout opens the path to the prior high and possibly new-high swing territory. 419 is the short-term momentum pivot; a close, or next session's open, below 419 closes the attack window and forces a return to the bottom-base coil for the next setup. Momentum is building now, but this is not yet a confirmed breakout.

META and AMZN are the strongest threads inside the Magnificent Seven. META pushed higher on volume, supported by paid subscriptions across three software products, a Meta One monthly-fee product, and expectations for future AI-agent monetization. 500–700 remains a long-term accumulation zone; 638–730 is overhead resistance, and a breakout unlocks a larger trend. AMZN is pushing up on volume with the prior pullback having shown light volume — price action is closer to a new-high trend, and trend-hold logic is clearer than chopping around short-term.

## 3 Trading Strategy

Don't conflate semiconductor fundamentals with short-term capital squeezes. AI earnings-revision upgrades still support a subset of chip and memory names, but call-chasing, crowding, and expanding-volume pullbacks will cause violent moves that have nothing to do with fundamentals. If SOX keeps selling off, the topping formation gets clearer; if capital keeps crowding into semis, IGV's squeeze fuel runs low. Conversely, once semiconductor capital exits, software may get a better rotation window.

On sizing: TSLA — use 419 as the swing-stop reference, and 465-and-above to confirm the breakout; don't blindly add size in the middle of the range. META and AMZN, as volume-backed strengthening names, can stay on watch for capital persistence. CRM, IGV, and the software complex suit waiting for low-volume pullbacks and capital switches. Energy — avoid if key levels break. Consumer, airline, and travel names that benefit from lower oil can be added to the rotation watch.
