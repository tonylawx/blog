0 · Market Read

The market looks strong on the surface, but the internal divergence is pronounced. The Nasdaq gained nearly 2% and the S&P about 0.8%, yet the S&P equal-weight index actually fell about 0.2%, with advancers clearly fewer than decliners, and the Dow also softened modestly. Capital is heavily concentrated in semiconductors, AI, and mega-cap tech, while traditional cyclicals, industrials, pharma, and consumer staples are not getting synchronized follow-through.

The short-term spine of the tape is still leadership-theme trading — not a broad risk-appetite repair. The Philly Semiconductor Index slope has gone very steep: daily RSI is near 97, with the streak hitting 18 consecutive up days — that is an extreme overbought zone. Weekend Middle East negotiating headlines, Hormuz-related news, and next week's flow shifts can all move the rhythm. This is not a tape to simply treat as a one-sided grind higher.

1 · Risk Watch

The biggest risk is that semis have run too far, too fast. Right now this looks more like capital crowding into the leadership than a stable state of long-term trend acceleration. Strength does not mean it can't pull back. The current SOX slope is very hard to sustain for long, and once the short-term chase energy in the hot sector gets exhausted, a pullback remains entirely possible.

Macro and flows also warrant caution. The US-Iran talks over the weekend carry variables — the make-up of the US delegation, the deployment of a third carrier group, and the progress of intermediary mediation can all swing next week's opening sentiment. Next week's pension rebalancing could bring $2-billion-plus of equity-selling pressure, and CTA buying is cooling noticeably versus the prior two weeks. Without a new positive catalyst to step in, flows are not friendly to the index.

2 · Names to Watch

AMD ripped about 13.9%, the price is near 350, and it's inside the previously-set target zone. A heavy-volume long-body candle says short-term momentum is still strong, but with the position overly concentrated it's not the moment to keep getting greedy. Wait for confirmation of a bottoming structure on volume after the rip — that can serve as the take-profit signal. On a 30–40x high-growth multiple, the 2027 forward ceiling is around 397; for higher upside, the company needs to actually deliver on its longer-cycle targets.

NVDA reclaimed 208 and closed at an all-time high. The last resistance in front of the prior high at 212 has been cleanly cleared, and a heavy-volume long-body candle says the trend keeps pushing higher with no clear technical overhead for now. TSM got a lift after Taiwan raised the single-stock fund holding cap from 10% to 25%, giving bullish flows room to add — combined with strong fundamentals, still suited to holding for more upside. MSFT is still in its uptrend structure, but the bounce is on declining volume and the RSI top divergence hasn't fully repaired — short-term explosive power needs more base-building. TSLA sits around 376–380, where post-earnings options positioning adjustments make the tape lean neutral — the direction needs more time to grind out. LLY — on news of competition in oral weight-loss drugs, the multiple carries de-rating risk; start watching below 900, with 710–778 the more important support zone. Only sub-700 gets closer to "undervalued."

3 · Trading Strategy

The current strategy is not to chase the highs blindly, but to run position management around the strong core book. NVDA and TSM have healthy trends and can be held with the trend. MSFT still targets 500+, but don't expect an explosive move too quickly. AMD is in the target zone — if the position is light, wait for a structure-confirmation before closing the book for profit; investors running heavy concentration should be reducing exposure to the same sector.

Next week's trading puts headlines and flows on equally weighted footing. If the weekend talks go smoothly and Hormuz risk eases, cyclicals can catch up and tech has a shot at staying strong. If the talks stumble and flows soften, the pullback pressure on the hot semis gets more pronounced. Operationally: keep the core strong-asset exposure, avoid adding big into extreme overbought conditions, and wait for pullbacks, support zones, and volume confirmation before deploying new risk.
