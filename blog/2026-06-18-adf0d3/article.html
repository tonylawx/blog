0 · The Fed pivots hard hawkish: forward guidance abolished, stagflation risk front and center

At this meeting the FOMC did not just hold the benchmark rate unchanged. It unanimously rewrote the policy statement and scrapped forward guidance entirely. That pulling-the-rug move ran straight through market expectations and triggered violent asset-price moves. The new language is blunter: it explicitly attributes part of the inflation overshoot above the 2% target to supply shocks lifting prices in sectors like energy. It also upgrades the labor-market assessment, noting payrolls are growing in line with labor-force growth. Geopolitical uncertainty notwithstanding, the statement still stresses that economic activity is expanding at a solid pace, and adds "productivity gains and strong capital investment" to the language. This directness signals the Fed's policy weighting has decisively shifted back to inflation, not employment.

The dot plot and the Summary of Economic Projections (SEP) sent a more dangerous long-term signal. The 2026 federal funds rate median jumped from 3.4% to 3.8%; 2027 and 2028 moved up to 3.6% and 3.4% respectively; the longer-run neutral rate held at 3.1%. In other words: high rates will last longer than the market expected, and inflation returning to target may be pushed past 2028. More worrying, the 2026 core inflation forecast was revised higher while real GDP was revised lower. Once a slowing economy and sticky inflation cross paths, stagflation risk rises materially. The dot plot median points to one hike next year, but the dissent is severe: five participants project two hikes, one projects three. Market pricing followed fast and now fully prices at least one hike in 2026. Under the new Chair, the Fed will also revisit its communications framework, balance sheet, and new data sources (e.g., AI's impact on productivity); the dot plot's role as forward guidance will be heavily diluted and may even exit the stage by year-end.

1 · Indiscriminate index selloff: watch triple-witching flows; back to range trading

Hit by the hawkish statement, all 11 SPX sectors were routed; 430 of the index's constituents fell, only 72 rose. SOXX (Philadelphia Semis) was not spared either, with more than half its names closing red. Looking at the tape, the moment the statement hit at 2 p.m. the market went straight down, then had a brief bear-trap bounce during the press conference, before Powell's hawkish tone triggered another wave of concentrated selling into the close. After-hours, a macro/geopolitical headline sparked a pulse-higher rip in index futures, but this kind of news-driven short-term volatility has no solid logical foundation. Chasing it is a good way to get hurt.

Tomorrow brings triple-witching options expiry and settlement, which will further amplify disorderly volatility. During these windows where longs and shorts squeeze each other around option strikes, a lot of intraday up and down action is just institutional repositioning and flow, not deep fundamental logic. Stay disciplined; if you cannot read the short-term tape, sit out. Do not try to find a reason for every candle, and do not engage in high-volatility short-term games here. Wait patiently for the post-settlement directional choice, which is the prudent play.

3 · Discipline on high-valuation leaders: ISRG and NFLX, no bottom-fishing before a stabilization pattern

Recent laggards ISRG and NFLX have sparked plenty of "is it time to buy the dip" debate, but discipline must outrank the bottom-fishing impulse. On long-term logic, ISRG is the monopoly behind the da Vinci surgical system and has an extremely deep moat. In healthcare, when patients can afford it, they choose the safer, better-recovery top-tier equipment; earnings certainty is high and the long-term "doesn't trap buyers" reputation is well earned. But long-term logic does not erase short-term valuation risk. The stock has long commanded an extremely rich forward PE, while current revenue growth is only mid-single digits. High valuation plus growth headwinds demands a real margin of safety on the trade.

ISRG shows zero sign of stabilization, the price pressing toward $320. Below $405, until a powerful stabilization pattern prints, do not enter. Even if it loses $320, as long as the structure has not flattened, stay in cash. NFLX faces similar valuation pressure; its 2026 forward PE median sits at $80, the 2027 floor at $77. The stock has already broken below $77 into the "strike zone," but yesterday's ugly intraday tape showed zero fight from buyers. For an initial position, strict risk control is the first rule. Below $77, without a clear bottom structure, I will not pull the trigger; even if a buy setup emerges, single-name position size stays hard-capped under 4%.

4 · SpaceX's new casino opens; the lockup approaches, and the megafund "collar" reveals the defensive tell

SpaceX had its first down day since listing: a 13% intraday range, turnover near 36%, full-day volume lower but still close to 140 million shares. Tomorrow weekly options list; the options market's rampant speculation will amplify underlying volatility, and weekly options become a meaningful variable. With the first post-IPO insider trading window (day 70, August 20) approaching, flow is starting to expose extreme caution from large holders.

The unusual options flow shows early-stage whales, unable to sell in the spot market, are building "collar" structures at scale. For example, an institution bought tens of thousands of September $200 puts while selling the same number of September $220 calls. With the underlying held, this is a textbook defensive hedge, giving up potential upside over $220 to insure against a large drop below $200. While the crowd agonizes over "missing out," big money is already building walls against tail risk. Technically, SpaceX is at a bearish advantage below $200; the next key support drops to $190 and $180. With weekly options listing alongside month-end expiry tomorrow, two-way squeeze volatility is guaranteed, so watch whether the stock can defend $180.
