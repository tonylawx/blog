## 0. Market Read

The short-term tone is bullish, but that optimism is built on the premise that the Middle East conflict doesn't deteriorate further. The market is more likely to chop sideways at the highs or grind modestly higher than to roll over into a deep pullback. JPM nudging its 2026 S&P target from 7200 up to 7600 further reinforces the backdrop of AI premium still lifting risk appetite.

Three things underpin the short-term bullish bias. First, the market's expectation that the US and Iran ultimately reach a deal via negotiation is warming, with the Strait of Hormuz reopening still the most probable path. Second, downside protection in options is cheap, which tells you positioning is not paying up for a fast drawdown. Third, the buyback window has reopened and short covering is still happening, providing clear downside cushion for the index. Overall, this is more "bullish-leaning chop at the highs" than one-sided acceleration.

## 1. Risk Watch

The biggest risk still comes from headline whipsaw, especially sudden shifts between ceasefire extensions, Strait blockades, and the negotiating rhythm. The moment the geopolitical situation re-escalates, the current "stuck-at-the-highs" thesis breaks quickly, and the market can flip directly from bullish-leaning chop into risk-release mode.

The second layer of risk comes from valuation and earnings validation. JPM's target bump mostly reflects a re-lift in AI sentiment and risk appetite — the neutral measurement range is still around 7400, which tells you 7600 looks more like sentiment premium than a number already locked in by earnings. With big-tech prints landing densely over the next two weeks, if the earnings, guidance, or AI-monetization proof disappoints, the market can easily rotate from "chasing the rally" into "pullback correction."

## 2. Names to Watch

The first focus is the software ETF **IGV**. The key signal level has been nudged up from 88.6 to 88.9, because when the tape touched 88.8 intraday it printed heavy volume. If IGV subsequently closes or opens decisively above 88.9, it would trigger further short covering and provide more solid right-side confirmation of an uptrend in software.

The second focus is **ISRG** and **MSFT**. ISRG has no add plan right now — only a break below 430 would be worth reassessing for a cleaner trend buy point; until then it's more of a high-level consolidation range and not something to chase. MSFT is clearly waiting on the IGV breakout to confirm — if software as a group turns stronger, Microsoft's odds of a right-side leg improve in lockstep; on a pullback, the downside can still be treated as a long-term scale-in zone.

## 3. Trading Strategy

At this stage it's better to run trigger-based conditional trades than to chase emotionally. At the index level, you can anchor the strategy to the premise that the geopolitical situation holds: as long as the conflict doesn't escalate further, treat the short-term tape as bullish-leaning chop at the highs; the moment the headlines flip, prioritize tightening risk exposure — don't fight the headline.

At the sector and single-stock level, prioritize watching whether IGV cleanly clears 88.9. If the breakout holds, follow through on the right side in the direction of software and a broad risk-appetite thaw; if it fails, keep treating it as a range and don't rush to chase. ISRG — don't front-run; reassess the risk/reward below 430. MSFT — even on a breakout of the key zone, it's better to wait for confirmation before stepping in rather than firing the moment price touches the level.
