0 — The Month-End V Is Pretty, but Direction Still Has to Wait

The last few sessions of June are when U.S. equities are easiest to get wrong. The four majors diverged — Nasdaq led, Russell lagged, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index recouped all of the prior day's loss. The intraday action was a dive followed by a sharp recovery, as if the tape got dented and then forcibly popped back out. It looks strong on the surface, but underneath it is still the sauce of month-end rebalancing, quarter-end repositioning and second-half portfolio reshuffling.

More tellingly, the one-year rolling correlation between the equal-weight index and the S&P 500 has dropped to roughly 79%, well below the long-term average of 96%. The U.S. market is no longer trading like a single index — it is a rotation among memory, software, space-concept names and a handful of mega-cap tech stocks, each taking turns telling the story. Moves in this phase don't necessarily reflect true direction; we need this week to finish before it becomes easier to see where new-quarter capital actually wants to stay.

1 — Memory Is Still Hot, but Semis Are Being Tested on Valuation Discipline

The AI chain has rotated from GPUs to power, to data centers, to CPUs, and now to memory — the hotspot keeps moving. The cleanest contradiction in memory right now is this: long-term supply will expand, but the short-term shortage persists. Korean memory giants are pushing a new wave of massive capacity buildout, with the goal of pushing capacity materially higher over the next several years. For the supply chain, that relieves the shortage over the long term; for stock prices, distant water won't quench a near-term thirst.

That is also the most delicate spot for MU. Earnings expectations are still being revised higher, but what's trading today is the fiscal 2027 peak — and once that window keeps getting pushed out, capital starts asking whether fiscal 2028 estimates can keep climbing too. AVGO still has high-growth support, so its premium multiple is defensible; MRVL faces more direct valuation pressure, and if the current support band doesn't hold, the distance to the next support is meaningfully wider. Semis are tradeable — you just can't treat every bounce as an unconditional continuation.

2 — Space Is a Catalyst Trade; Mega-Cap Moves Are Positioning Noise

Space concept stocks ran hot today, with SPCX and RKLB both drawing chasing flows. SPCX's near-term logic comes from index inclusion, expected passive buying and the imagination premium around potential M&A talks; RKLB recaptured key levels in one move, also driven by news catalyst and a high-volatility risk appetite. But for these bounces you have to separate time horizons — there's real overhead trapped supply, and how long passive buying can keep the bid depends on whether buy-side intensity can absorb the unbagging flow.

GOOG's bounce looks more like month-end positioning noise. It had been trading ugly, then reclaimed the decline today with index-related passive buying layered on — but that kind of up-down move doesn't necessarily reflect a fundamental change. TSLA has rebounded fast over the past two sessions, but it's now running straight into the heavy resistance zone at 419–465, and the range is getting compressed. Looking up from the 370 area, the truly tradeable swing width has narrowed — the closer to the middle, the less it makes sense to chase back and forth.

3 — Whether Software Takes the Baton Comes Down to the Key Level on IGV

There's been a subtle shift in software recently. IGV saw a notable short-covering episode in late May; since then, short interest has come down but remains elevated by historical standards. The bounce over the last couple of days looks more like dip-buying at support plus some short profit-taking — not enough on its own to confirm a brand-new leg. Software has been so weak for so long that a lot of capital is still in the long-semi / short-software pair trade.

The level to watch is 94.5. Only a decisive break above that gives IGV a higher probability of quickly tagging 101+; until then, treat it only as a bounce off support. Today's tape can be lively, but the direction hasn't been set. Month-end positioning noise, index passive flows, theme catalysts and short covering are all entangled — the easiest environment for false signals. The steadier play is still to wait for new-quarter capital to reposition itself.
