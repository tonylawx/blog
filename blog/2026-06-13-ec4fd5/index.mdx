## 1 — Nasdaq-100's "Rebalancing" Logic: Cull the Weak, Keep the Strong, Bet on AI and Space

In the adjustment effective June 22, Nasdaq-100 adds five names — ALAB, CRWV, NBIS, RKLB, and TER — while removing CHTR, CTSH, INSM, VRSK, and GS. GS had retail following but its results kept disappointing, and it was ultimately culled. Of the five newcomers, RKLB is a space concept; the other four all tie to semiconductors and AI infrastructure. Nasdaq's committee is accelerating the inclusion of the market's hottest themes to keep its earnings-growth edge.

Behind this is the structural logic of long-term index upside: the S&P and Nasdaq committees regularly remove companies whose growth has slowed or whose results are weak, and add fast-growing or stable compounders. The underlying EPS of the constituents is continuously optimized. Owning an index ETF means passively riding this selection mechanism — the hidden engine that keeps the long-term Nasdaq trend pointed northeast.

## 3 — SpaceX Day One

IPO priced at $135, raised $75 billion, market cap reached $1.77 trillion — but only 556 million shares were floated, just 4.252% of total shares. This ultra-low float is expected to last at least two months. It opened at $150, traded as high as $176.52 intraday, and closed at $160.95 — a first-day gain of 19.22% on turnover of $84.6 billion and a churn rate of 93.76%. Huge capital rotated in and out repeatedly, an extremely speculative signature.

An ultra-low float plus the imminent listing of options could trigger structural shifts. Once weekly options arrive — possibly as soon as Tuesday (June 16) — concentrated call buying could force dealers to buy underlying to delta-hedge, producing a gamma squeeze. This is the same mechanism behind explosive moves in growth names before. In addition, inclusion in the Nasdaq is expected around July 6; passive inflows should further compress the actually available float.

The lockup release schedule will gradually break the low-float regime in the second half: after the Q2 report (around August) up to 20% of locked shares may release; then 7% each at days 70, 90, 105, 120, 135; after the Q3 report (around November) an additional 28%; full unlock at day 180 (around mid-December). In other words, the share price for the first two months is priced entirely on sentiment and liquidity — decoupled from fundamental valuation. Space-concept stocks were already drained sharply on Day One; if SpaceX keeps siphoning speculative liquidity, related shadow stocks face continued outflow pressure.
