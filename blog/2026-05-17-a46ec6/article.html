0 Market Assessment

All four major indices pulled back in sync, each down more than 1%. Decliners clearly outweighed advancers in the S&P 500, and energy was the only sector to close green out of the eleven. The late-session flush looks more like an options-settlement-driven liquidity shock layered on long liquidation than a confirmed new bear leg. The S&P, Nasdaq, PHLX Semiconductor (SOX), and SMH have only returned to their first layer of support — whether the trend actually reverses depends on whether those levels give way.

The core tension heading forward is the tug-of-war between Treasury yields and AI earnings expectations. Semiconductor earnings revisions remain the primary support for index EPS, but the rapid rise in the 10-year yield has entered the warning zone. If yields stay elevated or push toward 5%, tech valuations will be persistently pressured and the index will most likely enter a choppier range. If yields roll over, the AI leadership theme has room to extend.

1 Risk Watch

The biggest short-term risk is not the down day itself, but the crowding from prior call buying and chase positions. Once the rally fails to extend, options expire, profit-taking and stop-loss orders hit simultaneously, and the capital that was driving the move higher turns into selling pressure — a classic long unwind. When the slope of an advance is too steep without proper handoff, the chip structure usually needs to be repaired with either time or a pullback.

The Treasury yield is the most important leading variable to monitor over the coming weeks. If the 10-year breaks the downtrend in place since 2023 and stays high, equities will show more pronounced risk-off behavior, with singles vulnerable to up-one-day-down-sharply-the-next volatility. As yields approach the 5% area, the market's sensitivity to Fed verbal intervention and expectation management rises materially.

2 Names in Focus

NVDA — next week's earnings are a major checkpoint for the AI thesis. The quarter just passed is unlikely to be the main issue; the key is whether forward guidance can keep beating expectations. The technical concern is that the run has been too fast, leaving short-term overbought conditions in need of repair. A pullback below 211 becomes a more attractive level for fundamental capital to re-engage. As long as the forward guide is not materially disappointing, the slow-grind-higher structure remains intact, and above 236 there is room to keep extending.

MSFT remains the best risk-reward core weight in software. The valuation band continues to point to 441–592 next fiscal year, with a median near 517. The current pressure comes more from the broad shorting of the software complex than from any fundamental deterioration. TSLA is still pinned near resistance — a tactical trim by swing capital is understandable, but the SpaceX IPO and potential equity arrangements introduce two-sided uncertainty: a gap breakout or a bearish fade. On a bearish catalyst, below 368 becomes the next swing-watch zone.

3 Trading Strategy

At the index level, do not rush to define a one-day pullback as a trend reversal. S&P 500 watch 728–735; only a loss of 728 qualifies as a meaningful pullback. Nasdaq watch 688–698; SOX watch 489–507; SMH watch 533–552. As long as these first-layer supports are not decisively broken, the strategy is to focus on the quality of the pullback and the direction of yields — do not act on end-of-day panic.

At the sector level, prepare two scripts. If semiconductor speculative enthusiasm continues and yields pull back, the index can repair and extend the AI theme. If high-beta semiconductor capital begins to exit, the broader market comes under pressure, but software may attract rotation flows given cheap valuations and short-squeeze risk at six-year highs. Operationally, avoid undisciplined chasing inside crowded semis, and place IGV, MSFT, and unusual strength in software leaders at the top of the watch list.
