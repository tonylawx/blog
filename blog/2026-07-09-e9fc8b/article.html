While some agencies have revised their capex estimates upward, the actual guidance from megacap tech will be the ultimate reality check.

1 · FOCUS

Meta Initiates AI Price War; Commercialization Battle Begins

META staged an impressive reversal. After opening down over 3%, the stock surged on high volume to close up more than 4%, marking a 7% intraday swing. This high-volume bullish candle confirms strong demand. News broke that META signed long-term agreements for network equipment and storage, planning to double its computing capacity next year, while aiming to produce its own AI chips starting in September.

More importantly, META released Llama 3.1 with a new paid tier for enterprise developers, marking the first time the company has charged businesses for its model. The pricing strategy is extremely aggressive: it is priced at only 25% of the cost of top-tier models from OpenAI and Anthropic. Despite the bargain price, benchmarks show Llama 3.1’s performance is highly competitive, putting direct pressure on competitors. This aggressive pricing will force U.S. competitors into a strategic reassessment of their monetization pathways.

Technically, after breaking above the 596-611 platform, META’s pullback yesterday confirmed the validity of the support, and today’s rally solidified the rebound. The price is currently trading near its 200-day moving average (rising to $642 after-hours). If it stabilizes here, the next target is $680. However, a significant lock-up zone exists between $690 and $730; a true long-term uptrend requires breaking above this range. Long-term positions are best held with a cost basis closer to $500, but success depends on one's confidence in AI monetization.

2 · FOCUS

Starbucks to Build Internal AI Tools: Minor Direct Impact on Microsoft and IBM

Reports indicate Starbucks SBUX is developing proprietary internal AI tools to manage inventory and maintenance, intending to replace software currently sourced from Microsoft MSFT (inventory management) and IBM (maintenance management). These tools are scheduled for testing by the end of next year.

While this appears to challenge the dominance of enterprise software providers, the financial reality is negligible. Starbucks plans to trim its budget by $30 million this fiscal year, with software spending cuts accounting for only $10 million and professional services/outsourcing cuts accounting for $13 million. For a consumer giant with a market capitalization exceeding $120 billion, a $10 million reduction in software spend will have no material impact on Microsoft or IBM's financials. This is a sentiment-driven headline rather than a fundamental threat.

Valuation-wise, Starbucks SBUX has a forward target cap of 49-86 for September 2026 and 62-108 for September 2027. The current stock price of $106 is trading near the upper limit of its 2027 forward valuation. However, after September, the forward valuation window shifts to 2028. Combined with its upwardly revised five-year compound growth rate of 21%, this valuation premium should dissipate over the coming months. Technically, the stock remains stable with multiple support levels below and minimal resistance above, keeping the uptrend intact.

4 · SEMIS

Semiconductor Index Stabilizes: Reversal Requires a Breakout Above Key Resistance

The semiconductor sector (SOXX) closed back above 582 today. This indicates that the recent sharp sell-off has paused, and downward momentum has eased, marking a short-term stabilization.

However, stabilization is not a trend reversal. The key resistance for a renewed uptrend is 621. Until 621 is cleared, the current rise remains a technical rebound. Buying here is a trade-off: if correct, you secure a relative low; if incorrect, you risk buying at the peak of a short-term bounce. The boundaries are clear: 582 is the level for stabilization, while 621 is the confirmation for trend reversal.
