0 · MACRO

· Macro and Rate Pressure

US equities faced broad selling pressure across all four major benchmarks throughout the session. Early dip-buying quickly faded into noon selling, and despite a brief mid-afternoon bounce, stocks closed near session lows. Crucially, the familiar market rotation broke down: technology and semiconductor shares declined in tandem. Within the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index, only 20 stocks closed in positive territory, led primarily by memory names such as MU and CRD. As sector rotation failed to buffer losses, risk-off sentiment surged. Even with VIX hovering near 18, two core macroeconomic drivers shifted dramatically over the past week.

The first catalyst was energy prices spiking on geopolitical escalation. Red Sea shipping disruptions coupled with Middle East conflict escalation drove Brent crude intraday above $102 per barrel before closing past $100. Crude breaking triple digits amplified inflation concerns. The second driver was labor market resilience. Initial jobless claims dropped to 187,000—a multi-decade low—while the four-week moving average fell to 207,500 and continuing claims shrank to 1.796 million.

Strong labor data paired with rising oil prices reinforced hawkish rate expectations. Interest rate futures fully priced in a 25 basis point rate hike this year, with a second hike probability crossing 50%. The 10-year Treasury yield climbed to 4.697%, topping the May 20 high of 4.685%. If bond shorts push yields toward 5%, equity valuations face severe downside pressure.

1 · EARNINGS

· Earnings and Single Stocks

INTC reported second-quarter revenue of $16.1 billion, topping the $14.42 billion consensus. Adjusted earnings per share hit $0.42 against $0.21 expected, while foundry revenue grew 31% year-over-year to $5.8 billion. Third-quarter guidance also came in ahead of expectations, with revenue projected between $15.8 billion and $16.8 billion and adjusted EPS at $0.38.

Despite beat-and-raise metrics, the stock failed to hold gains. INTC surged 13% after hours before retreating sharply to a modest 1.45% gain and settling near 3%. From a valuation standpoint, even with raised estimates, 2027 expected EPS of ~$1.50 puts the stock at 66.6x forward P/E at $100—far above historical levels when EPS exceeded $4 between 2019 and 2021. Technically, INTC lacks near-term support, with its 200-day moving average down near $65 and overhead resistance at $103–$105 and $115–$120. The post-earnings blow-off top signals distribution; strict position sizing and stop losses remain essential.

LMT posted Q2 EPS of $7.94 on $20.1 billion in sales, easily beating Wall Street estimates of $7.19 and $19.3 billion. Backlog reached a record $230 billion. Full-year 2026 guidance was revised higher to $30.30 EPS and $80.8 billion in revenue.

While geopolitical friction fuels defense demand, contract timing depends on Congressional appropriations. A pending $87.6 billion supplemental request includes $67.1 billion for the Department of Defense. The House passed a $95 billion reconciliation package by a slim margin, but Senate approval faces bipartisan hurdles. LMT popped 10% post-earnings, breaking above its multi-month base and 200-day moving average, putting valuation near a neutral 2026 midpoint target of $595. Defense names offer long-term stability, but budgetary friction prevents rapid semiconductor-style rallies.

2 · MARKET

· Index Technical Levels

QQQ previously broke key support at $693, leaving chart structure in a bounce-and-fade posture. The Nasdaq has avoided an accelerated selloff primarily because the S&P 500 has not yet broken its core technical level.

SPY currently trades near $738, with $727 serving as the critical line in the sand. A breakdown below $727 would trigger a broader index correction of roughly 5%, pushing prices toward the 200-day moving average near $695. Under a severe downside scenario, secondary support rests between $630 and $672.

The SOXX semiconductor ETF remains stuck in a breakdown-and-test pattern. Initial support lies near $532. Should SPY fail at its key level, SOXX faces a retest of $532, representing a 9% to 10% drawdown from current levels, with secondary risk opening a 16% drop.

3 · CAPEX

· AI Capex and Long-Term Thesis

GOOG delivered solid quarterly operating metrics, but a $15 billion year-over-year surge in capital expenditures drove quarterly free cash flow negative for the first time in company history. Market anxiety over elevated capex and monetization timelines pressured the stock.

GOOG is testing key support at $298. A breach would open downside toward $257. Big Tech capex trends dictate supply chain sentiment: semiconductor names reacted negatively to GOOG's budget limits, while hyper-scalers face squeezed cash flows alongside rising borrowing costs.

Over a longer horizon, Big Tech free cash flow is projected to trough in 2026, stabilize in 2027, and re-accelerate in 2028. Long-term index buyers must weigh whether massive AI investments yield expected monetization. If monetization succeeds, market pullbacks present disciplined accumulation opportunities; if ROI falls short, rate pressures will force broader equity repricing. Maintain a disciplined, risk-managed stance.
