0 Market Assessment

The core narrative around US equities continues to revolve around AI capex. The prints and guidance from GOOG, AMZN, META, and MSFT show that megacap tech remains committed to heavy AI infrastructure spend in 2026. Short-term volatility does not change the medium-term main thesis, but divergence will become more pronounced: companies with cash-flow-backed investment more easily earn valuation premiums, while those under cash-flow pressure require stricter verification of the payback cycle.

At the index level, it remains a market where risk appetite is being propped up by a handful of heavyweight tech names, with the semiconductor chain still tethered to capex expectations from the cloud hyperscalers. As long as AI orders, capex, and compute demand do not clearly reverse, GOOG, AMZN, META, MSFT, and the SOXX/SMH complex remain the primary windows through which the market prices this theme.

1 Risk Warning

The biggest risk lies in the tension between capex upward revisions and free cash flow burn. Accelerating AI spend can support revenue expectations and the compute-chain boom, but if cash flow is rapidly consumed and the payback period lengthens, the market will rotate from "willing to give narrative-driven valuations" to "interrogating profit delivery," and high-multiple tech stocks will be the first to come under pressure.

The second risk is that post-earnings expectations have already been re-raised. Any subsequent adverse change in orders, gross margin, cloud growth, depreciation/amortization, or regulation could amplify volatility. For semiconductor ETFs and leading individual names, after short-term sharp runs, it is unwise to chase undisciplined at peak sentiment.

2 Names to Watch

Focus remains on the four cloud-and-AI heavyweights: GOOG, AMZN, META, and MSFT. GOOG and MSFT are more about validating the cloud and model ecosystem; AMZN needs to be watched for AWS growth and the cash-flow pressure from capex; the key question for META is whether the advertising cash cow can continue to cover AI infrastructure investment.

On the semiconductor side, watch the chain tied to AI servers, GPUs, ASICs, HBM, and advanced packaging — AMD, SOXX, SMH, and related names. If the hyperscalers continue to revise capex upward, semis still have fundamental support; but rhythmically, distinguish between trend positions and short-term trades — do not treat earnings sentiment as risk-free upside.

3 Trading Strategy

Tactically, do not easily fade the AI main thesis, but also do not blindly add exposure in the high-volatility zone after earnings sentiment releases. The more reasonable approach is to keep core positions and wait for pullbacks to key moving averages, retests of prior highs, or post-earnings digestion, then scale into GOOG, AMZN, META, MSFT, and the semi ETFs in tranches.

Risk control takes priority over pursuing single-day P&L. If an individual name shows a high-volume breakdown of key support, or its earnings logic is falsified by deteriorating cash flow, trim first rather than averaging down; if the index continues to be led higher by AI heavyweight names, stay with the strongest leaders and use position caps and stop levels to control drawdown.
