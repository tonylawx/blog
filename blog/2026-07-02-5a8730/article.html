0 · SEMIS

The Semiconductor Pullback Has Not Confirmed an End Yet

Today's tape was not a simple index move. SPX finished almost flat, NDX led lower, and DJI rose instead. More importantly, S&P 500 breadth improved meaningfully, with 356 advancers versus 145 decliners, while defensive sectors such as health care, consumer staples and utilities began to lead.

The real pressure is still in semiconductors. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index was sold for a second straight day, with every component down. The key zone I had marked was 554 to 582; today's intraday low reached 554.91, and the index closed near 566, so it barely held the edge. If 554 breaks next week, treat it as a confirmed topping signal and shift into pullback-bottoming mode. The two downside observation zones remain 489 to 532 and 450 to 467.

The single-stock split is also clear. MRVL has confirmed a break below its 254 to 271 weakening zone, falling nearly 10% today; technically, the next leg lower now matters, and a move below 211 is possible. MU and SNDK are harder to call simple breakdowns because the prior rally was too steep and there was too little overhead volume built above. For now, they are more about feedback from emotional money and leveraged positioning. Whether they stabilize depends not only on fundamentals, but also on whether short-term buyers can absorb the selling pressure.

1 · RISK

A 30% Pullback Call Is Worth Watching, but It Is Not a Conclusion

One focus of market discussion is Michael Burry's short direction on semiconductor-related names. The group includes NVDA, AMAT, CAT and TSLA, with the first three tied to the AI capex chain. Semiconductors, equipment, power and infrastructure are all effectively part of the same AI trade.

His view of a potential 30% pullback in the Philly semi index roughly maps to the 450 to 467 area. That level is not absurd, because it overlaps with the second downside observation zone I had already marked. But in trading, "visible" is not the same as "inevitable." There is still 554 first, then 489 to 532. Every layer can produce stabilization, a rebound, profit-taking or stop-loss flow.

Semiconductors are not unsupported by earnings. The real problem is that some names ran too fast and now need ownership to change hands again. The farther prices fall, the worse the risk-reward does not necessarily become; if the index truly reaches 450 to 467, chasing fresh shorts there may actually have less meaning.

2 · RISK

Thinner Liquidity Is the Biggest Hidden Risk Right Now

The most important thing to watch now is not any single stock, but market liquidity. U.S. equity volume was heavy in June, with average daily turnover reaching 23.3 billion shares; June 18 and June 26 reached 33.0 billion and 32.0 billion shares, respectively. But book liquidity in SPX mini futures fell roughly 33% month over month, from $12 million to $8 million.

That creates a dangerous structure: volume is larger, but real depth at each price level is thinner. On the way up, large orders can push prices quickly; on the way down, selling can drive prices much deeper just as fast. The violent intraday moves of the past month come from this logic.

This is also why I would not rush to chase defensive stocks or low-level rebounds. Institutional capital is indeed moving toward health care, staples and utilities, but whether this is a second-half style rotation or only short-term risk-off positioning still needs to be confirmed by the market reaction after a semi breakdown. If semis break and other sectors only dip slightly or continue rising, then it will look much more like a real style rotation.

3 · SETUP

Trade the Ranges, Not the Emotion

TSLA was a classic "buy the expectation, sell the news" case today. Q2 deliveries were 480,126, above the market's 406,600 expectation, yet the stock still fell about 7.5%. The market had already front-run the delivery beat over the past few days, pushing price quickly toward strong resistance near 430; once the news landed, traders took profits. Now it returns to the lower end of the range to wait for the next swing opportunity.

META gave back a lot today, but as long as the pullback stays above 559, it can still be treated as post-breakout digestion. The real warning is a break below 551, which would send it back into the lower range. MSFT now depends on whether it can clear the 392 to 435 right-side resistance band. If it cannot, it remains range-bound; if it can, momentum capital may return.

NVDA is relatively resilient inside the semi complex, and volume has not expanded meaningfully, so for now it looks more like sideways digestion. If AI capex growth slows in the future, it will be affected, but moving from hypergrowth to normal growth is not the same as the thesis ending. AMD can also be treated as sideways for now, with 490 to 520 as first support; if semis break, its weaker overhead positioning still deserves attention.

4 · SETUP

Wait for Confirmation Next Week; Do Not Rush to Fill Positions

The trading discipline is simple: 554 is the first line for the Philly semi index. If it holds, there is still room for a rebound and repair; if it breaks, treat the top as confirmed and watch 489 to 532, then 450 to 467. Names like MRVL that have already broken cannot still be imagined as strong stocks; for MU and SNDK, watch whether emotional and leveraged flows keep trampling each other.

Defensive names and prior weak stocks can go on the watchlist, but there is no rush to chase. NFLX and CRG both rose well today, and they had already released a round of selling pressure, so their positioning is relatively less dangerous. But with liquidity thin and semis not yet confirmed stable, I would rather wait for a little more right-side confirmation than force myself into an uncomfortable position just to capture a few extra points.

After the long weekend, second-half style will be repriced. Earnings season begins in mid-to-late July, and any change in mega-cap tech capex needs close attention. The most important thing now is not to guess the final direction, but to wait for the market to confirm it with price: are semis only washing out short-term holders, or entering a deeper pullback; are defensive sectors only a risk-off shelter, or the start of a new leadership line?
